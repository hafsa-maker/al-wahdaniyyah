Foundation

Basics of Islam

Islam is the complete way of life revealed by Allah (سُبْحَانَهُ وَتَعَالَى) to humanity through the Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ). It rests on clear foundational principles that guide every aspect of a believer's life.

01
الشَّهَادَة
Shahada — Declaration of Faith

The testimony that there is no god worthy of worship except Allah, and that Muhammad (ﷺ) is His final Messenger. This is the gateway into Islam and the most fundamental act of faith.

02
الصَّلاة
Salah — The Five Daily Prayers

Prayers performed five times daily (Fajr, Dhuhr, Asr, Maghrib, Isha) that establish a direct connection between the servant and his Lord, forming the spiritual backbone of a Muslim's day.

03
الزَّكاة
Zakah — Obligatory Charity

An annual purification of wealth given to those in need. It cleanses the soul from greed and strengthens community bonds by ensuring the welfare of the less fortunate.

04
الصَّوْم
Sawm — Fasting in Ramadan

Complete abstention from food, drink, and wrongdoing from dawn to sunset during the blessed month of Ramadan. It cultivates taqwa (God-consciousness) and deep spiritual discipline.

05
الْحَجّ
Hajj — Pilgrimage to Makkah

The once-in-a-lifetime pilgrimage to the sacred city of Makkah, obligatory for every Muslim who is physically and financially able. It is the greatest gathering of humanity in submission to Allah.

The Heart of Islamic Belief

Tauheed — The Oneness of Allah

Tauheed (التَّوْحِيد) is the most essential concept in Islam — the absolute, uncompromising belief in the uniqueness and oneness of Allah. It is the reason creation exists, the message of every Prophet, and the foundation upon which all worship is built.

"The Prophet (ﷺ) was sent for ten years in Makkah calling people to Tauheed before any other obligation was revealed — a testament to its supreme importance."

Scholars of Islam have categorized Tauheed into three interlocking dimensions, each necessary and none sufficient alone. Together, they constitute complete and authentic monotheism:

🌌 تَوْحِيد الرُّبُوبِيَّة
Tauheed al-Rububiyyah

Oneness of Lordship.

Allah alone is the Creator, Sustainer, and Sovereign of all existence. He controls life and death, gives and withholds, and governs every atom in the universe. Nothing happens except by His will. Even the polytheists of Makkah acknowledged this aspect — yet it was not enough for salvation.

🤲 تَوْحِيد الأُلُوهِيَّة
Tauheed al-Uluhiyyah

Oneness of Worship.

All acts of worship — prayer, supplication, sacrifice, vows, hope, fear, and love — must be directed exclusively to Allah. This is the meaning of لَا إِلٰهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ. Directing any act of worship to other than Allah is Shirk, the gravest sin in Islam.

📖 تَوْحِيد الأَسْمَاء وَالصِّفَات
Tauheed al-Asma' was-Sifat

Oneness of Names & Attributes.

Affirming all the beautiful names and attributes Allah has affirmed for Himself in the Quran and Sunnah, without distortion, denial, comparison to creation, or asking "how." Allah's attributes are perfect and unlike anything in creation.

Why Tauheed is the Supreme Priority

إِنَّ اللَّهَ لَا يَغْفِرُ أَن يُشْرَكَ بِهِ وَيَغْفِرُ مَا دُونَ ذَٰلِكَ لِمَن يَشَاءُ

"Indeed, Allah does not forgive association with Him, but He forgives what is less than that for whom He wills." — Quran 4:48

Tauheed is the purpose behind the creation of jinn and mankind (Quran 51:56), the message of every Prophet from Adam to Muhammad (ﷺ), and the first right that Allah has upon His servants. All other acts of worship derive their value from the sincerity of one's Tauheed.

Iman

The Six Pillars of Faith

Islamic belief (Iman) encompasses six foundational articles that every Muslim must believe in the heart, affirm with the tongue, and act upon with the limbs.

☝️
Belief in Allah

Firm conviction in the existence and oneness of Allah — His names, attributes, and Lordship over all creation. He has no partners, sons, or equals.

👼
Belief in the Angels

Angels are noble creatures made of light who obey Allah perfectly. Among them are Jibreel (who brought revelation), Mikael, Israfeel, and Izraeel.

📚
Belief in the Divine Books

Allah revealed scriptures to His Prophets. The Quran, revealed to Muhammad (ﷺ), is the final and perfectly preserved word of Allah, abrogating all previous scriptures.

🕊️
Belief in the Prophets & Messengers

Allah sent over 124,000 Prophets to guide humanity. Muhammad (ﷺ) is the seal and final Messenger. Prophets include Adam, Ibrahim, Musa, Isa, and Muhammad (peace be upon them all).

⚖️
Belief in the Day of Judgement

Every soul will be resurrected and held fully accountable for its deeds. Allah will judge with perfect justice. The righteous enter Jannah; the disbelievers face Jahannam.

🌿
Belief in Divine Decree (Qadar)

Everything — good and evil — occurs by the knowledge and will of Allah. He decreed all things 50,000 years before creation. This belief brings tranquility: nothing happens outside His wisdom.

Sources of Knowledge

The Quran & Sunnah

The two primary sources of Islamic knowledge and law — both divinely inspired and inseparable.

الْقُرْآن الْكَرِيم
The Holy Quran

The literal word of Allah, revealed in Arabic to the Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ) over 23 years through the Angel Jibreel. It contains 114 Surahs and over 6,000 verses covering theology, law, history, ethics, and guidance for all of humanity until the Day of Judgement. Memorized by millions across the globe, it remains unchanged since its revelation.

السُّنَّة النَّبَوِيَّة
The Prophetic Sunnah

The sayings (hadith), actions, and tacit approvals of Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ), preserved meticulously through chains of narrators. The Sunnah explains, expands, and contextualizes the Quran. Key hadith collections include Sahih al-Bukhari, Sahih Muslim, and the four Sunan. Allah commands obedience to the Prophet as obedience to Himself.

سُورَةُ الْإِخْلَاص
Surah Al-Ikhlas · Chapter 112

The Surah of Pure Monotheism — equivalent in reward to one-third of the Quran. The clearest declaration of Tauheed in all of scripture.
